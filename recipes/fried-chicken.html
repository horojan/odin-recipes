Fried Chicken

Photo by Shardar Tarikul Islam on Unsplash

Description

This fried chicken batter yields the crispiest, spiciest, homemade fried chicken I have ever tasted! It is equally good served hot or cold and has been a picnic favorite in my family for years.

Ingredients

  • One (1) of quart vegetable oil for frying
  • Four (4) of cups all-purpose flour, divided
  • One (1) ½ tablespoons of garlic salt
  • One (1) tablespoon of ground black pepper
  • One (1) tablespoon of paprika
  • ½ teaspoon of poultry seasoning
  • One (1) ½ cups of beer, or as needed
  • Two (2) egg yolks, beaten
  • One (1) teaspoon of salt
  • ¼ teaspoon of ground black pepper
  • One (1) whole chicken, cut into pieces

Steps

  1. Heat oil to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C) in a deep-fryer.
  2. Mix three (3) cups flour, garlic salt, one (1) tablespoon pepper, paprika, and poultry seasoning in a medium bowl.
  3. Stir together 1 1/2 cups beer, remaining 1 1/3 cups flour, egg yolks, salt, and 1/4 teaspoon pepper in a separate bowl; thin mixture with more beer if the batter is too thick.
  4. Moisten chicken pieces with water, then dip in the dry mixture. Shake off excess and dip in the wet mixture, then dip in the dry mix once more.
  5. Fry chicken pieces in hot oil until well-browned and internal temperature reaches 165 degrees F (74 degrees C), 15 to 18 minutes.
  6. Remove chicken pieces and drain on paper towels before serving.
